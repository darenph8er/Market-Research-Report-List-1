<p><p>The dextrin market is primarily divided into maltodextrin and cyclodextrin segments. Maltodextrin is a polysaccharide commonly derived from starch, widely used as a thickener, filler, or preservative in food and beverage products. Cyclodextrin, on the other hand, is a cyclic oligosaccharide known for its ability to encapsulate flavors and nutrients, enhancing product stability and solubility. Both types serve essential roles in various industries, including food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics, contributing to product functionality and consumer appeal.</p></p>
