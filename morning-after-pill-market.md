<p><p>The morning-after pill market comprises two primary types: Levonorgestrel and Ulipristal Acetate. Levonorgestrel, a progestin, is available as an over-the-counter option effective if taken within 72 hours post-intercourse. Ulipristal Acetate, an emergency contraceptive pill, offers an extended window of effectiveness up to 120 hours and requires a prescription in some regions. Both types serve the same purpose—preventing pregnancy after unprotected intercourse—but differ in their mechanisms, time frames, and accessibility, catering to diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
