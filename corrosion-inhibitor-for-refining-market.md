<p><p>Corrosion inhibitors in the refining market are classified into two main types: organic and inorganic corrosion inhibitors. Organic corrosion inhibitors are carbon-based compounds that form protective films on metal surfaces, effectively reducing corrosion rates. They are often used in various refining processes due to their effectiveness and compatibility with other chemicals. In contrast, inorganic corrosion inhibitors are composed of mineral salts and oxides, providing a robust protective barrier against corrosion, particularly in harsher environments, and are typically more cost-effective in large-scale applications.</p></p>
