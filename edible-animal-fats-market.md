<p><p>The edible animal fats market consists of three primary types: liquid, solid, and semi-solid. Liquid fats, such as lard and fish oil, are commonly used in cooking and food processing for their high smoke points and flavor. Solid fats, like tallow, are primarily used in baking and frying, providing texture and stability to products. Semi-solid fats, often derived from blends, offer versatility for applications such as margarine and spreads, balancing between the characteristics of liquid and solid forms.</p></p>
