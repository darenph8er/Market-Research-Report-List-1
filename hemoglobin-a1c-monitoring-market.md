<p><ul><li>Ion-exchange</li><li>High-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC)</li><li>Immunoassay</li><li>Boronate affinity chromatography</li><li>Direct enzymatic assay</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Hemoglobin A1c monitoring market consists of various analytical techniques for measuring glycated hemoglobin levels, crucial for diabetes management. Ion-exchange chromatography separates hemoglobin variants based on charge. High-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) offers high precision and rapid results. Immunoassay utilizes antibodies for specific detection. Boronate affinity chromatography selectively binds to glycated hemoglobin, while direct enzymatic assays provide fast, straightforward measurements. Each method varies in sensitivity, specificity, cost, and ease of use, catering to different clinical and laboratory needs.</p></p>
