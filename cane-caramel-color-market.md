<p><p>The cane caramel color market is categorized into four main types based on production methods and characteristics. Class I, plain caramel color, is produced without additives, resulting in a mild flavor and light color. Class II, caustic sulfite caramel color, is made using sulfites, offering a darker hue with enhanced stability. Class III, ammonia caramel color, employs ammonia in its process, providing a rich color and flavor. Class IV, sulfite ammonia caramel color, combines sulfites and ammonia, yielding deep coloration and stability in food applications.</p></p>
