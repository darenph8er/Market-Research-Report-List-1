<p><p>The Hollow Fiber Ceramic Membrane market is segmented into Ultrafiltration, Microfiltration, and Nanofiltration based on pore size and application. Ultrafiltration membranes have medium-sized pores, effectively separating macromolecules, proteins, and colloids. Microfiltration membranes feature larger pores, ideal for removing suspended solids, bacteria, and particulates. Nanofiltration membranes have the smallest pores, enabling the separation of divalent ions, organic molecules, and small particles while allowing monovalent ions to pass. Each type caters to specific industrial, water treatment, and purification needs, offering varying levels of filtration precision.</p></p>
