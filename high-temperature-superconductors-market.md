<p><p>The high-temperature superconductors (HTS) market is divided into two primary types: first-generation (1G) and second-generation (2G) superconductors. First-generation HTS, primarily based on yttrium barium copper oxide (YBCO), are known for their relatively lower critical current density and mechanical properties, limiting their application. In contrast, second-generation HTS utilize innovative deposition techniques to create thin films with higher critical current densities, excellent performance, and flexibility, enabling their use in advanced applications such as power transmission and magnetic systems.</p></p>
