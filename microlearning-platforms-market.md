<p><p>Microlearning platforms exist primarily in two market types: cloud-based and web-based. Cloud-based platforms offer users access to learning content and tools hosted on remote servers, facilitating scalability, accessibility, and ease of updates. These are typically subscription-based, allowing users to access materials from various devices. Conversely, web-based platforms focus on delivering learning content directly through web browsers, requiring an internet connection but often allowing for a more straightforward implementation without the complexities of cloud infrastructure. Both types aim to enhance on-the-go learning.</p></p>
